Our History

Connecting Canadians with nature since 1939.

A Legacy of Conservation

From a small magazine celebrating natural wonders to a national voice for wildlife, Nature Canada has grown alongside the environmental movement in Canada. Explore key milestones below.

1930

Reginald Whittemore founds Canadian Nature magazine, the seed that will eventually grow into Nature Canada.

1940

First issues of Canadian Nature are placed in schools across Canada; mascot Bobby the Coon debuts.

1948

The Audubon Society of Canada is established, marking the start of organized advocacy.

1958

Canadian Nature is renamed Canadian Audubon with a stronger conservation focus.

1971

The organization broadens its mandate and becomes the Canadian Nature Federation (CNF).

1977

CNF helps found the Committee on the Status of Endangered Wildlife in Canada (COSEWIC).

1996

Launch of the Important Bird Areas (IBA) Program with Bird Studies Canada.

2004

CNF adopts the name Nature Canada, welcoming everyone who cares for nature.

2014

Celebrates 75 years of connecting Canadians to nature.

2025

Nature Canada continues to lead on nature-based climate solutions and the goal of protecting 30% of land & sea by 2030.
